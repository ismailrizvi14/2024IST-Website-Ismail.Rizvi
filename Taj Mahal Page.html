Taj Mahal

What is Taj Mahal?

The Taj Mahal is located on the right bank of the Yamuna River in a vast Mughal garden that encompasses nearly 17 hectares, in the Agra District in Uttar Pradesh. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal with construction starting in 1632 AD and completed in 1648 AD, with the mosque, the guest house and the main gateway on the south, the outer courtyard and its cloisters were added subsequently and completed in 1653 AD. The existence of several historical and Quaranic inscriptions in Arabic script have facilitated setting the chronology of Taj Mahal. For its construction, masons, stone-cutters, inlayers, carvers, painters, calligraphers, dome builders and other artisans were requisitioned from the whole of the empire and also from the Central Asia and Iran. Ustad-Ahmad Lahori was the main architect of the Taj Mahal.

Why visit Taj Mahal?

The Taj Mahal is a world heritage site in India and it is said to be one of the most beautiful buildings in the world. The Taj Mahal means ‘The Crown Of All Palaces’ in Persian. It is a very popular tourist attraction to visit in India and people all over the world travel to India just to see it. siting the Taj Mahal complex in India enables you to connect with a piece of ancient history in real life. Being there in person is a much more powerful experience compared to seeing pictures online or reading about it in a book. You can explore the Taj Mahal on your own, although it is advised to get a guided tour which may take 2-3 hours. In that way, you will be able to dive deeper into the history behind the Taj Mahal and be immersed in its atmosphere. The Taj Mahal continues to stand as a symbol of true and eternal love, even though it was built so many years ago for one emperor’s late wife. If you are travelling through India, the Taj Mahal is a must-see building to connect deeper to a more spiritual side of what may appear as just a famous, ancient, and popular tourist attraction. The whole area of the Taj Mahal is gorgeous. The mausoleum is made of bricks with white Makrana marble on the surface and the guest house and mosque are made from bricks and red sandstone. The walls of the Taj Mahal are covered in mosaics using precious stones and intricate designs.
